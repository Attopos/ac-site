On Craft and Character

Enduring work is not built on speed and noise, but on structure, rhythm, and restraint. Classical architecture teaches the same rule: proportion before ornament, system before spectacle.

When daily practice is documented, knowledge stops being scattered fragments. It becomes a corridor you can walk again, where each review lights another torch in your internal temple.
