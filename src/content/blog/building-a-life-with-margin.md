Most people do not fail because they lack goals. They fail because every part of life has already been allocated.

Margin is not laziness. It is spare capacity for thought, revision, and recovery. Without it, everything becomes reactive. The calendar fills, but the work that actually changes a life keeps moving outward.

A good system creates deliberate emptiness. That emptiness is where judgment returns.
