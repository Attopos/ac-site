# Blog Direction

## Positioning

This site should feel like a personal publication, not a portfolio landing page.

- Structure reference: Vercel Blog
- Tone reference: editorial, calm, credible
- Identity: a builder's archive for systems, leverage, and intentional living

## First Impression Goals

Within a few seconds, the visitor should understand:

1. This is a writing-first site.
2. The author has a point of view.
3. The archive is organized and worth exploring.

## Design Principles

- Lead with content, not biography.
- Use strong hierarchy and restrained color.
- Keep the interface clean enough for long-form reading.
- Let typography and spacing do more work than boxes and decoration.
- Keep one clear signature detail: warm editorial palette plus a precise grid.

## Information Architecture

### Home

- Intro with name, positioning, and a short statement
- One featured essay
- Latest writing list
- Topic clusters
- Small about strip

### Writing Index

- Archive header
- Featured entry
- Recent essays
- Notes or shorter entries
- Topic filters later

### Article Page

- Title
- Deck or subtitle
- Date, reading time, category
- Body with comfortable measure
- Related posts
- Back to archive

## Visual System

- Background: warm paper, not pure white
- Text: deep charcoal
- Accent: muted blue for interaction
- Serif for reading, sans for navigation and metadata
- Thin rules instead of heavy cards where possible

## Build Plan

1. Migrate to Astro for maintainable templates and Markdown content.
2. Create content collections for blog posts.
3. Build the home page, writing index, and article layout first.
4. Add projects and about after the writing system feels right.
